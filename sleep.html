Introduction to Sleep & Rest

Getting enough quality sleep is essential for maintaining good health and overall well-being. Sleep helps your body repair itself, boosts your immune system, improves mood, and enhances cognitive function. While the amount of sleep needed varies by individual, adults generally need 7-9 hours per night to function at their best.

Why is Sleep Important?

Sleep plays a vital role in many aspects of health. Adequate rest improves memory, increases energy levels, helps maintain a healthy weight, reduces stress, and lowers the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and high blood pressure. It also supports emotional well-being and cognitive functions, which are critical for daily activities and decision-making.

Quality sleep is essential for both physical and mental health. It is particularly important for seniors, as sleep disturbances can increase the risk of memory loss, cognitive decline, and weakened immune function. Understanding the importance of sleep and making it a priority can lead to better overall health.
